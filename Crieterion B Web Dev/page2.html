**Below is a List of Links that Will Send You to Numerous Donation Sites**


    Feed The Children

    Feed the Children is a non profit organization that recieves donations and uses them to feed children in developing countries. Some specific countries can be chosen when donating on this website.


    Save The Children

    Save the Children is another non profit organization that relys on donations to feed children in hunger. Donations to this site go only to children in Africa.


    Canadian Feed The Children

    This is a Canadian run charity that accepts donations and uses the money to establish good food security and save starving children. This site was named one of the Top 10 Impactful Charities in Canada in 2019 and donations go to a variety of developing countries.


    No Kid Hungry

    No Kid Hungry is a charity that has a goal to end child hunger around the world. They take monthly and one time donations and give lots of information on where their sponsors' money goes. Specified donating is not allowed and the charity decides where the money goes but the truly dedicated can fundraise for the charity and will be assisted.



**Below is a list of credible references and sources that can provide more information**


    UN Sustainable Development Goals

    There are many statistics and lots of useful information on this site. It encompasses hunger overall but the subsection of children can be chosen. Does not specifically mention urban farming but has notions of solutions.


    Canadian Feed The Children

    This is also a very informative website and focuses mainly on children in Sub Saharan Africa. Does not talk about Urban Agriculture but suggests other potential solutions.


    Urban Agriculture and Africa

    Focuses mainly on Africa but as this is one of the most affected region by child hunger, this website is very useful and informative.


    Waterplex: Can Urban Agriculture End World Hunger

    Focuses on the solution of Urban Agriculture in rich/developed countries. Is not specific to children but rather world hunger.
